On-Court Feel & Play Experience

Kitchen & Dinking

This is where the 16mm thickness earns its keep. The paddle absorbs pace cleanly — incoming heat from drives converts into soft, angled replies with less effort than thinner cores. My resets from the transition zone were noticeably cleaner. If you play a patient, dink-heavy game, you'll feel the payoff in the first session.

  • Dwell time is noticeably longer than the 14mm variant
  • Resets from both the kitchen and mid-court carry better placement
  • Soft drops feel more consistent — less "bouncy" off the face
  • Ernie and ATP attempts are aided by the elongated shape's reach
  • Third-shot drops land with confidence after a short break-in period

Drives & Power Play

Power sits at 6.5 for a reason. The thick core trades the trampoline snap of a 14mm pad for feel and control. Drives are clean and directional, but you're not overwhelming players with pace. If you rely on drives to manufacture points, this paddle will feel like a limiter.

That said — the directional accuracy on drives is a genuine strength. Placement is rewarded here. If your game is about putting the ball where you want it rather than how hard you can hit it, the tradeoff works.

Spin & Serve

At 1,820 RPM average, the raw carbon face sits in the upper-mid range. Not Ben Johns territory, but well above most fiberglass alternatives at this price. Slice serves bite well. Heavy topspin drives from the baseline generated consistent kick in the bounce — the combination of spin and dwell is a real advantage on high-arcing thirds.

Who It's Best For

The Hyperion CGS 16mm is right for a specific player. Don't buy it based on brand alone.

Buy If
  • You're a control-first player who lives at the kitchen
  • You want thermoformed construction without the power trampoline
  • You play a reset-and-dink game that rewards patience
  • You prefer a slightly head-light elongated setup
  • You're open to adding tungsten tape to dial in swing weight
Pass If
  • Your game depends on hard drives and overhead pace
  • You want max spin — the Ben Johns 16mm beats this clearly
  • You're a beginner without the mechanics to leverage the dwell
  • $189 without a demo doesn't sit right with you
Bottom Line

The Joola Hyperion CGS 16mm earned its spot for control-first players. It's the right thermoformed paddle for the wrong player if you're shopping on brand alone — but if you're willing to give up some pop for real dink feel, it delivers. The 16mm thickness isn't a gimmick here.
